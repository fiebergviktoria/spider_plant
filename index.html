Spider

Plant

General:
  • Names: Chlorophytum comosum, spider plant,spider ivy, ribbon plant, hen and chickens, Grünlilie
  • Description: grows up to 60 cm tall, the green leaves reach length of 20 to 45 cm and are 6 to 25 mm wide, flowers and plantlets are produced on branches, roots are succulent
  • Distribution: most of Africa, Australia, southeast of the USA, up to a height of 2500m on the kilimanjaro

Scientific classification:
  • Genus: Chlorophytum (from the greek words chloros (yellow green/light green) and phyton (plant))
  • Subfamily: Agavoideae (also including agaves from which for example Tequila is produced)
  • Family: Asparagaceae (based on the edible garden asparagus)
As a houseplant:
  • Care: easy because very robust plants, fluoride in water can lead to burnt looking tips, non-poisonous for animals and humans
  • Propagation: mostly through plantlets, which often already form roots in the air, plantlets without roots can be kept in water until the start to root
Varigations:
  • Chlorophytum comosum ‘Bonnie’ (curly, mid-green leaves with a central white stripe, white stems)
  • Chlorophytum comosum ‘Variegatum’ (dark-green leaves with white margins, green stems)
  • Chlorophytum comosum ‘Vittatum’ (mid-green leaves with a central white stripe, white stems, often in hanging baskets to display plantlets)
